## üîç Influencer Discovery Process

### Step 1: Build Target List (Use Tools + Manual Search)

**Tools:**
- **HypeAuditor** (hypeauditor.com) - Free trial, then $299/month
- **Upfluence** (upfluence.com) - Free trial for discovery
- **Modash** (modash.io) - $99/month, great for micro-influencers

**Manual Search Methods:**

**Instagram:**
1. Search hashtags: #guitartok #guitarlessons #guitartips #learnguitar
2. Check "Suggested" profiles on guitar accounts
3. Look at who's commenting on big guitar pages
4. Browse location tags (music stores, studios)

**TikTok:**
1. Search "guitar lessons" and filter by creators
2. Browse #GuitarTok top videos, check creators
3. Look at duets/stitches of popular guitar content

**YouTube:**
1. Search "guitar tutorial" and "guitar gear review"
2. Filter by upload date (last 6 months = active creators)
3. Check subscribers, views, engagement in comments
4. Look at "Recommended channels" on big guitar YouTubers

**Reddit:**
1. Check post history of active r/Guitar contributors
2. Find users who post high-quality content regularly
3. DM the best contributors (many have YouTube/Instagram links in bio)

---

### Step 2: Vet Influencers (Quality Check)

**Check Engagement Rate:**
- Formula: (Likes + Comments) / Followers √ó 100
- Target: >3% for influencers (>5% is excellent)
- Red flag: <1% (could be fake followers)

**Check Audience Authenticity:**
- Read comments (real humans or bots?)
- Check follower growth (sudden spikes = bought followers)
- Use tool like HypeAuditor's Audience Quality Score

**Check Content Quality:**
- Professional production value
- Consistent posting schedule (at least weekly)
- Positive, helpful tone (not toxic or controversial)

**Check Brand Safety:**
- No offensive content
- No competing brand partnerships (other guitar apps)
- Professional demeanor

## üìà Measuring Influencer ROI

### Track These Metrics

**Per Partnership:**
1. **Reach:** Total views/impressions of sponsored content
2. **Engagement:** Likes, comments, shares, saves
3. **Engagement Rate:** (Engagement / Reach) √ó 100
4. **Clicks:** Use unique link (bit.ly/fretpilot-[creatorname])
5. **Conversions:** App downloads or store purchases from their code
6. **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA):** Compensation / Conversions
7. **ROI:** (Revenue - Compensation) / Compensation √ó 100

**Tools:**
- **Bitly:** Track link clicks
- **Unique Discount Codes:** Track store purchases (e.g., MARTY10 for Marty Music)
- **UTM Parameters:** Track web traffic (fretpilotstudio.com?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=influencer&utm_campaign=marty)
- **Google Analytics:** See traffic from influencer links

### Good vs. Bad ROI

**Good Partnership:**
- CPA < $20 for app installs
- CPA < $100 for store purchases
- ROI > 2x (you make $2 for every $1 spent)
- Authentic, engaged comments on content

**Bad Partnership:**
- CPA > $50 for app installs
- No measurable conversions
- Low engagement on sponsored content (suggests fake followers)
- Negative sentiment in comments

### What to Do with Data

**If partnership performs well:**
- Book again for next quarter
- Increase compensation for exclusivity
- Ask for long-term brand ambassador deal

**If partnership underperforms:**
- Analyze why (bad content, wrong audience, timing)
- Don't rebook
- Focus budget on better-performing creators

## üö´ Red Flags to Avoid

### Don't Partner With:
1. **Fake followers:** Check with HypeAuditor or manually (weird usernames, no engagement)
2. **Controversial creators:** Offensive content could harm brand
3. **Inconsistent posters:** Haven't posted in 3+ months
4. **Competitors' partners:** Active partnerships with other guitar apps
5. **Poor production quality:** Blurry videos, bad lighting (reflects poorly on your brand)
6. **Demanding divas:** Unreasonable requests, rude communication

### Warning Signs During Negotiation:
- Asks for full payment upfront (50% upfront is standard)
- Refuses to sign agreement
- Wants to delete content after 30 days (you need longer exposure)
- Doesn't disclose other brand partnerships
- Guarantees specific results (no one can guarantee virality)

---

## üìû Influencer Communication Best Practices

### Response Times
- Reply to inquiries within 24 hours
- If negotiating, respond within 2-3 hours during business hours
- Set expectations: "We'll send product within 5 business days"

### Tone
- Professional but friendly
- Enthusiastic about their content (genuine compliments)
- Flexible on creative direction
- Clear on requirements (FTC disclosure, timeline)

### What to Provide
- High-res logos (PNG with transparent background)
- Brand guidelines (colors, tone, key messages)
- Product photos (if they can't shoot their own)
- Discount code details
- Talking points (but don't script them - authenticity matters)

### What NOT to Do
- Demand specific language ("You must say it's the best app ever")
- Micromanage creative (they know their audience)
- Get defensive if review is constructive
- Pressure for faster turnaround than agreed
- Disappear after they deliver content (thank them, reshare, stay in touch)
